The no-noise baseline performs best overall, with the shortest travel time, shortest travel distance, 100% completion, and the largest safety margin. Adding safety-value noise generally degrades task performance: agents take longer paths, complete fewer goals, and maintain smaller minimum distances. However, the safety filter still appears robust in the sense that conflict percentage remains 0% across all noise settings. The biased-noise cases sometimes recover performance compared to zero-bias noise, especially at std=0.03, but the overall trend is that uncertainty in the safety value reduces efficiency and goal completion while preserving collision avoidance in these short evaluations.
